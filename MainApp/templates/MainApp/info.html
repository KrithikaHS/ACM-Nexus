ACM Nexus 2.0
Unite Through Innovation

Overview

  • Theme:
    Innovation across multiple domains
  • Objective:
    Encourage creative solutions to diverse problem statements within the given themes.
  • Time:
    April 11th, 2025, from 11:00 AM to April 12th, 2025, 11:00 AM
  • Venue:
    KLS GIT, InfoTech building
  • Team size:
    4 members per team

Event Timeline

  • April 11th, 2025:
    • 11:00 AM: Event starts.
    • 2:00 PM: Round 1 of evaluation
    • o 7:00 PM: Round 2 of evaluation
  • April 12th, 2025:
    • o 9:00 AM: Round 3 of evaluation
    • o 11:00 AM: Event ends, project submissions close.
    • o 11:30 AM - 12:00 PM: Project presentations and judging.

General Rules

  • Team Formation:
    • Teams must consist of exactly 4 members
  • Eligibility:
    • All team members should belong to the same college.
    • Participants must abide by the event's Code of Conduct.
  • Project Development:
    • Projects should align with at least one of the provided problem statements
    • Use of open-source libraries and APIs is permitted

Submission

  • Each team must submit their project by the specified deadline
  • Submissions should include a presentation (slides or video) and a brief documentation describing the project.

Judging Criteria

  • Projects will be evaluated based on innovation, technical complexity, relevance to the problem statement, and presentation quality.

Code of Conduct

  • Respectful and ethical behaviour is expected from all participants
  • Plagiarism or using someone else's work without attribution is strictly prohibited

Disclaimer

ACM KLS GIT reserves the right to modify the event rules and guidelines if deemed necessary. Any changes will be communicated to the participants promptly

Phase 1
The first phase, Ideation & Problem Identification, will focus on brainstorming and conceptualizing project ideas. Teams will identify a real-world problem related to the hackathon theme and work on defining a feasible solution. During this stage, participants will submit an initial abstract or proposal outlining their approach, objectives, and expected outcomes.
April
11
Phase 2
In the Prototype Development phase, teams will begin building the basic structure of their solution. They will focus on implementing the core functionalities of their project, transforming their idea into a working prototype. Mentors will be available to provide feedback and guidance, ensuring that teams stay on track and refine their concepts as they progress.
April
12
Phase 3
The third phase, Refinement & Testing, will be dedicated to optimizing and debugging the project. Teams will work on improving the user interface (UI) and user experience (UX), fine-tuning their solution for efficiency and reliability. Rigorous testing will be conducted to ensure that the project functions smoothly, addressing any issues before the final phase..
April
12
Phase 4
In the Final Presentation phase, teams will showcase their project to a panel of judges. They will explain their approach, the challenges they faced, and the impact of their solution. A live demonstration or a well-structured presentation will help convey their idea effectively, ultimately determining their standing in the competition.
